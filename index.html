How to Calculate 3D Printing Costs?

Whether you are a hobbyist using a Prusa, Ender, or Bambu Lab printer, or running a 3D printing business on Etsy, knowing your exact costs is essential. Our free 3D print cost calculator helps you break down expenses beyond just the filament.

The Key Factors in 3D Print Pricing

To get an accurate price, you need to consider three main components:

  • Material Costs: Filament prices vary depending on whether you use PLA, PETG, ABS, or high-end carbon fiber materials. Our tool calculates the cost per gram based on your spool price.
  • Electricity Consumption: Long prints can consume a significant amount of power, especially with high-temperature heated beds. Our calculator uses a standard rate of $0.30/kWh (which you can adjust in the code if needed).
  • Maintenance & Wear: Don't forget that nozzles, PTFE tubes, and build plates are consumables that eventually need replacing.

Tips for Reducing 3D Printing Expenses

To save money, consider optimizing your infill settings or using "Draft" modes for non-functional parts. Reducing print time not only saves filament but also significantly lowers your electricity bill.
